Stump Grinding

Stump Grinding in Montgomery County, MD

Stump grinding is the fast, affordable way to get rid of an unwanted tree stump. Using a powerful grinding machine, we chip the stump down to 8–12 inches below ground level so you can reclaim the space, replant, or just stop mowing around it. It's our most popular service — and the most cost-effective way to clear a stump.

How stump grinding works

Our operator positions a stump grinder over the stump and uses a rotating carbide-tipped wheel to grind the wood into chips, sweeping back and forth until the stump is well below grade. The process is contained, relatively quiet, and far less disruptive than digging the stump out. Most single stumps are done in well under an hour.

What you get

  • The stump ground 8–12 inches below the surrounding grade
  • Upper root flare ground down so it won't re-sprout
  • Wood chips spread as mulch or hauled away with topsoil backfill
  • A level, tidy area ready for grass, garden, or landscaping
  • A clean job site — we sweep up and respect your property

Why grind a stump?

Left in place, stumps are tripping hazards, attract termites and other pests, sprout unwanted new growth, and waste usable yard space. Grinding eliminates all of that quickly and affordably — without the big hole and landscape damage that full extraction can cause.

Stump grinding vs. stump removal

People often search for "stump removal" when what they really want is stump grinding — the two terms get used interchangeably. Grinding chips the stump away below grade and leaves the deeper roots to decompose naturally, which is the right call for the vast majority of homeowners. If you want every last wood chip gone so you can drop in topsoil, sod, or a brand-new tree, just add our cleanup & haul away service. Got problem roots cracking a driveway or walkway? That's where root removal comes in.
