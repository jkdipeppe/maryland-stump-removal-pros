Stump Grinding & Removal FAQ

Everything Montgomery County homeowners ask us about stump grinding, removal, cost, and cleanup. Still have a question? Call or text 301-355-3769.

How much does stump grinding cost in Maryland?

Stump grinding cost depends on the diameter of the stump, the hardness and age of the wood, the number of stumps, and how accessible they are. Most single-stump residential jobs in Montgomery County are very affordable, and the price per stump drops when you have several done at once. We give free, upfront quotes with no obligation — call or text 301-355-3769 with a photo of your stump for a fast price.

What's the difference between stump grinding and stump removal?

Most people use the two terms to mean the same thing — getting rid of a stump. Technically, stump grinding uses a machine to chip the stump down to about 8–12 inches below ground level, leaving the deeper roots to decompose naturally. It's fast, affordable, and the right choice for the vast majority of homeowners, which is why it's our specialty. If you want every wood chip cleared out afterward so you can drop in topsoil, sod, or a new tree, we add our cleanup & haul away service.

How deep do you grind a stump?

We typically grind stumps to 8–12 inches below the surrounding grade — deep enough to plant grass, lay sod, or install new landscaping over the area. If you're planting a new tree or shrub in the same location, let us know and we can grind deeper or recommend full stump removal instead.

Can I plant grass after stump grinding?

Yes. Once the stump is ground below grade we can backfill the hole with the wood grindings or with clean topsoil so the area is level and ready to seed. For the best lawn results we recommend backfilling with topsoil before seeding, since fresh wood chips can temporarily tie up nitrogen in the soil.

What happens to the wood chips and debris?

That's your choice. We can spread the grindings as natural mulch around your beds and tree rings, or we can haul everything away and backfill with topsoil for a clean, level finish. Either way we always leave the work area cleaner than we found it.

Do you remove the roots too?

Standard stump grinding removes the stump and the upper portions of the major roots; the deeper, smaller roots are left to decompose naturally over time. If surface roots are cracking your driveway or creating tripping hazards, our dedicated root removal service targets those problem roots directly.

How long does stump grinding take?

A single small-to-medium stump usually takes 15–45 minutes. Larger stumps or hardwoods can take one to three hours, and multiple stumps are handled more efficiently per stump. We'll give you a time estimate with your free quote.

Is stump grinding safe for my property?

Yes. Our operators are trained and fully insured, and we locate utilities before we grind — we always recommend calling 811 (Miss Utility) to have underground lines marked before any grinding. We work carefully around fences, walkways, sprinkler heads, and landscaping to protect your property.

Do you handle large or multiple stumps?

Absolutely. We grind everything from a single backyard stump to dozens on a commercial lot, HOA common area, or large rural property. We bring commercial-grade equipment sized to the job and offer volume pricing for multi-stump projects.

Are you insured?

Yes, we carry liability insurance on every job. That protects you and your property and is something every reputable stump grinding company should be able to confirm before work begins.

Do you offer emergency or storm-damage stump removal?

Yes. After storms and high winds we prioritize urgent jobs where a fallen tree or stump is blocking a driveway, walkway, or living area. Call 301-355-3769 and we'll schedule your emergency stump removal as quickly as possible.
