How Much Does Stump Grinding Cost in Maryland?

What a stump costs to grind, the factors that change the price, and how to get an accurate quote in minutes.

It's the first question almost everyone asks: what's this going to cost me? The honest answer is that stump grinding is priced per job, because no two stumps are the same. But you don't have to fly blind — below is how pricing actually works in 2026, the things that move the number up or down, and the fastest way to get an exact price for your stump.

How stump grinding is usually priced

Most stump grinding is priced one of two ways: by the diameter of the stump (measured across the widest point at ground level), or as a flat per-stump rate. As a general industry guideline in 2026, per-inch rates commonly fall somewhere around $5 to $10 per diameter inch, and many single residential stumps land in the $100 to $500 range. Most companies also have a minimum charge — often around $200 — because it costs the same to load up and drive out whether your stump is small or large.

Those are ballpark national figures, not a quote. Local demand, access, and the specifics of your stump can push the real price above or below them. The only number that matters is the one you get for your stump.

What changes the price

Stump diameter

Bigger around means more wood to grind, so diameter is the single biggest cost driver. A slender ornamental stump is quick; a wide old oak or maple takes real time.

Number of stumps

This is where you save. Once we're on site, each additional stump is much cheaper than the first — often a small fraction of the price — because the travel and setup are already covered. If you've got several, grinding them in one visit is by far the best value.

Wood hardness and age

Hardwoods like oak grind slower than softer species, and a freshly cut stump can behave differently than one that's been sitting and drying for years.

Access and location

A stump in an open front yard is straightforward. One behind a narrow gate, on a slope, tight against a fence or foundation, or surrounded by landscaping takes more care and time — which can affect price.

Root flare and surface roots

Some stumps have large surface roots radiating out that you may also want ground down. If roots are lifting a driveway or walkway, that's a separate consideration — see our root removal service.

Cleanup and haul-away

Grinding a stump produces a surprising volume of wood chips. Leaving them in place as mulch is typically included along with cleanup and backfill; having everything hauled off is typically an add-on service. We cover this in our cleanup & haul away service, and it's worth it if you plan to plant.

Ways to keep the cost down

  • Bundle your stumps. Get all of them done in one visit for the best per-stump price.
  • Clear the area first. Moving rocks, planters, and yard clutter away from the stump saves setup time.
  • Be flexible on cleanup. Keeping the grindings as mulch instead of hauling them away costs less.
  • Send a clear photo. A good photo (with a measuring tape or shoe for scale) gets you an accurate quote without a wasted trip.

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to grind one stump?

Most single residential stumps fall somewhere in the $100–$500 range in 2026, with a typical minimum charge around $100. Your exact price depends on the stump's diameter, the wood, and how accessible it is. Text a photo to 301-355-3769 for a precise quote.

Is it cheaper to grind multiple stumps at once?

Yes, significantly. The first stump covers the travel and setup; each additional stump in the same visit costs much less. If you have several, doing them together is the best value by far.

Does the price include cleanup?

Leaving the wood grindings in place as mulch is typically included. Hauling all the chips away and backfilling the hole with topsoil is an add-on — that's our cleanup & haul away service.

Why do you charge a minimum fee?

It costs the same to load equipment and drive to your property whether the stump is small or large. The minimum makes small single-stump jobs worthwhile to schedule.
